# Why have small-caps drawn down more than the index?

Published: April 17, 2026

## What happened
The Nifty Smallcap 250 is down **14.2% from its Jan 2026 peak** versus the Nifty 50 which is down 4.5%. The divergence widened sharply through March as retail flows into small-cap MFs slowed and SEBI's stress-test disclosures prompted some schemes to raise cash.

## Why it happened
1. **Valuation reset**: Small-cap forward P/E went from 34x to 26x. Still not cheap, but less stretched.
2. **Earnings disappointments**: ~38% of small-caps missed consensus in Q4 FY26 vs 22% for large-caps.
3. **Flows**: Small-cap SIP inflows fell from ₹6,100 cr/month (Dec 2025) to ₹4,200 cr/month (March 2026).
4. **Liquidity stress**: Higher bid-ask spreads amplified price moves on modest volume.

## What we are telling clients
- This is a **valuation-driven correction**, not a credit or earnings collapse.
- Small-caps have historically seen drawdowns of 20-30% every 2-3 years. This is within that envelope.
- **Do not SIP-stop**. Do not lump-sum either. Stagger additions over 6-9 months if you are under-allocated.
- If small-cap exposure is >25% of equity portfolio, trim to 15-20% — concentration risk is the real issue, not direction.

## Who is most affected
Clients with aggressive profiles and concentrated small-cap SIPs. See individual portfolio notes for rebalancing actions.
