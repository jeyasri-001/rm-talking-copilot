# CIO Outlook — Q2 2026

## House view
We are **neutral on Indian equities** after the Q1 correction. Valuations have compressed from 22x to 19x forward earnings on the Nifty 50, closer to the 10-year average. Earnings revisions have bottomed for large-caps but continue to drift lower for small-caps.

## Asset class preferences
- **Equities**: Overweight large-cap, neutral mid-cap, **underweight small-cap**. Quality and earnings visibility matter more than momentum in this phase.
- **Fixed income**: Overweight. We expect 50bps of rate cuts over the next 9 months. Duration bias — prefer 5-10y gilt exposure and AAA corporate bonds.
- **Gold**: Tactical overweight at 5-8% of portfolio. Geopolitical premium + real-rate tailwind.
- **International**: Maintain 10-15% exposure via feeder funds or GIFT City route. US valuations stretched but earnings resilient.
- **Alternatives**: Selective private credit AIFs offer 11-13% net yields with modest illiquidity premium.

## Sector calls
- **Overweight**: Private banks, IT services (post-correction), pharma.
- **Neutral**: Consumer, industrials.
- **Underweight**: PSU capex plays, high-valuation new-age tech.

## Risks we're watching
1. Persistence of small-cap drawdown if retail flows reverse.
2. Rupee depreciation if Fed pivots later than expected.
3. Monsoon deviation — currently forecast as normal.

## Implication for client portfolios
- **Aggressive profiles**: Do not add to small/mid-cap on weakness without a plan. Consider tactical trim to 15-20% mid+small exposure.
- **Balanced profiles**: Use the debt overweight to increase duration (5-10y gilts).
- **Conservative profiles**: Stay the course. Consider locking in current gilt yields via target maturity funds.
