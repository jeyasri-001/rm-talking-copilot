# Product note — US equity via feeder funds & GIFT City

## Why US allocation
- USD diversification against rupee depreciation.
- Exposure to businesses absent in Indian markets (megacap tech, semis, biotech).
- Historical correlation of 0.52 with Nifty — genuine diversification.

## Approved routes
1. **Domestic feeder funds** (LRS-free): Nippon India US Equity, Motilal Oswal S&P 500, ICICI Pru US Bluechip. Simple, SIP-able, treated as debt for tax (indexation post 3y).
2. **GIFT City AIFs** (LRS within limit): Direct USD exposure via IFSC-based schemes. Tax-efficient; minimum typically $150k equivalent.
3. **Direct overseas** (LRS): Up to $250k/year per individual via LRS window.

## Suitability
- Recommended 10-15% of equity allocation for clients with AUM > ₹1cr.
- Aggressive younger clients can go up to 20% — longer compounding horizon.
- Conservative clients: skip or cap at 5%.

## Risks
- **Currency risk** — partial hedging available in some schemes.
- **Tax** — feeder funds are taxed as debt (slab rate post indexation removal); direct LRS may trigger TCS.
- **Concentration** — if large RSU exposure exists (e.g. tech employees), de-emphasise US tech.

## Compliance flag
For clients with RSUs/ESOPs in US tech companies, **avoid stacking Nasdaq 100 feeder funds on top** — concentration risk already significant.
