# Product note — Long-duration gilt funds

## Why now
With the RBI expected to cut the repo rate by 50bps over the next 9 months, long-duration sovereign paper (7-15y) offers attractive capital-gain potential alongside stable carry.

## Approved funds on our shelf
| Fund | Avg maturity | YTM | Expense ratio |
|------|-------------:|----:|--------------:|
| SBI Magnum Gilt Fund | 11.4y | 7.18% | 0.46% |
| ICICI Pru Long Term Bond | 12.8y | 7.24% | 0.69% |
| Bharat Bond ETF 2031 | 6.2y | 7.05% | 0.0005% |

## Suitability
- **Conservative clients**: Core fixed-income allocation (30-50%). Prefer the ETF for lower cost and transparency.
- **Balanced clients**: Tactical overweight 10-15% of portfolio for the duration trade.
- **Aggressive clients**: Optional barbell leg against equity.

## Risks
- **Duration risk**: A surprise 50bps hike would knock ~6% off NAV for 12y funds.
- **Mark-to-market volatility**: Not suitable for <18-month horizons.

## Talking points
1. "We're not chasing yield — we're positioning for rate cuts. The capital-gain leg typically adds 3-5% over 12 months when rates fall."
2. "If you need the money in under 18 months, we'll use a short-term fund instead."
3. "The ETF format (Bharat Bond) is tax-efficient and transparent — no manager risk."
