# ESOP / RSU diversification playbook

## The problem
Clients working at US-listed tech companies (FAANG, unicorns with dual listing) often have **>30% of net worth in single-stock equity** via RSUs and ESOPs. Even if the company is high-quality, this is idiosyncratic concentration risk.

## Rule of thumb
- <10% of net worth in single-name — fine.
- 10-25% — actively plan diversification on each vest.
- \>25% — treat every vesting event as a **sell-and-diversify trigger** unless client explicitly acknowledges the risk in writing.

## Mechanics
- **Sell on vest**: Pre-commit a % (say 50-70%) to sell on each vesting date. Removes emotion.
- **10b5-1 equivalent for Indian employees**: Most companies have blackout windows; plan transactions within trading windows.
- **Tax**: RSU vesting is treated as salary income at FMV — already taxed. Subsequent sale attracts capital gains only on the delta.

## Diversification targets
1. Indian large-cap equity MF (low correlation with US tech).
2. Debt / target maturity funds for stability.
3. International ex-US exposure if needed.
4. **Avoid** doubling up on Nasdaq 100 feeder or thematic US tech funds.

## Talking script
> "The goal isn't to bet against your company — it's to protect the wealth you've already earned. If you wouldn't use your cash bonus to buy more of your own stock, you probably shouldn't keep 100% of vested RSUs either."

## Compliance
If the client is an insider or has access to material non-public information, any transaction must follow the employer's trading window / pre-clearance policy. Document confirmation in CRM.
